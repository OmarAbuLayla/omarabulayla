Projects & Research Work

EMG-Based Speech Recognition

This research project investigates sentence-level EMG-based speech recognition using surface electromyography, with an emphasis on cross-subject generalization and robustness. The work integrates feature representation learning and deep learning models to evaluate system performance under realistic inter-subject variability and noise conditions.

The project involves several interconnected research components:

  • Feature Representation Learning: Evaluation of time-frequency and wavelet-based feature extraction methods for EMG-based speech recognition, focusing on robustness and generalization.
  • Neural Architectures for Biosignal Classification: Design and assessment of recurrent and transformer-based models for EMG time-series classification, with attention to training stability and cross-subject performance.
  • Robustness and Noise Analysis: Experimental analysis of system behavior under noise and subject variability to improve reliability and generalization.

Methods: EMG, MFSC, wavelet scattering, RNNs (GRU/LSTM), Transformers, cross-subject evaluation, noise augmentation
